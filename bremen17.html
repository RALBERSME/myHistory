Press releases


06th Sept. 1939

Il Popolo D'Italia:

Two large German steamers, the "Bremen" and the "Columbus", were able to reach safety in the neutral port of
Vera Cruz

08th Sept. 1939

Tidens Tegan / Oslo:

Informed sources report that the Bremen is safe in a neutral port in
Icelandic waters

09th Sept. 1939

Gazet van Antwerpen / Belgium:

The Bremen is said to be safe in theport of Reykjavik in Iceland . Since Denmark remains neutral, the ship apparently entered the port on Thursday morning.

09th Sept. 1939

Gazet van Antwerpen / Belgium:

The Bremen has not arrived at the port of Reykjavik in Iceland

12th Sept. 1939

Lloyd Anversois / Belgium:
The "Nieuwe Rotterdamsche Courant" writes that two days after leaving New York, i.e. on the open sea, the Bremen was placed under the Italian flag. Under the Italian flag, the ship set course for the Italian peninsula.

15th Sept. 1939

Aftonbladet / Stockholm:
The rumor that the Bremen is on its way to an Italian port is considered very unlikely in maritime circles. Even if this were the case, it is expected that the British would seize it and take it to a British port.

15th Sept. 1939

Gazet van Antwerpen / Belgium:

Netherlands Allgemeen Handelsblad writes: The Bremen is said to be in a Spanish port. The rumors that the ship passed into Italian hands at sea, was dismissed as unfounded by Norddeutscher Lloyd.

17th Sept. 1939

Aftonbladet / Stockholm:

It is now feared that the Bremen has crashed because a life preserver bearing the Bremen's name washed ashore on the coast of Massachusetts.

21st Sept. 1939

De Telegraaf, Amsterdam / Netherlands:

According to reports received in London this morning, the German steamer Bremen arrived in a British port under escort of warships. Official confirmation could not be obtained.
