Monday, August 28th, 1939:

At around 6 p.m. the S.S. Bremen anchors in the harbor of New York, but she has not yet reached her goal.


Urgent! - Instructions from the captain -



« All passengers, including the five German diplomats, are to leave the ship here. We will not take on any passengers. The ship must be cleared as quickly as possible, the paperwork and customs formalities must be completed, and fuel oil refueled.

We want to set sail back to Bremerhaven at six o'clock tomorrow morning.
The S.S. Bremen has to be back in Germany as quickly as possible. We don`t know what`s coming. »
